Tallinnmy hometown

Tallinn is a unique city where the medieval Old Town, with its ancient towers and cobblestone streets, meets modern skyscrapers. Here you can enjoy a rich cultural scene, taste Estonian food, and explore beautiful parks and the seaside – a perfect destination for history and nature lovers alike.

Childhood Favorites

Lasnamäe, Tallinn's largest district, is known for its Soviet-era architecture, characterised by a wide range of apartment blocks. While this may seem sombre, it holds great significance for me, as I spent my childhood exploring its unique corners.

The Tallinn Natural History Museum is a fantastic place full of discovery and excitement. It was my favorite spot as a child, where my family and I would go on weekends and school holidays. I loved exploring nature and its mysteries, and afterward, we often enjoyed delicious cakes in the café. This place brings back good memories of time spent with my family.

Tallinn's Old Town was a special place for me in childhood, as going "to town" often meant heading there. It was full of charming spots, small shops, and cafés, where it was exciting to step inside and explore. For a child, it was always new and interesting, with so many details to notice.
